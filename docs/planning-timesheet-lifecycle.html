Illustrated lifecycle guide

Planning, timesheets, consolidation and snapshots

This page shows the data lifecycle from two angles. Employees submit one weekly timesheet that can span multiple projects. Managers turn quote scope into weekly planning, approve or reject submitted time, re-estimate the remaining work, and freeze the closed week into an immutable snapshot.

Weekly employee submission window is global Employee POV: draft → submit → resubmit while open Manager POV: plan → validate → re-estimate → freeze Month end closes the last week then creates a snapshot

Lifecycle at a glance

How one month advances week by week
1. Sold baseline

Validated quote

Quote lines define the sold scope. Task + profile days and rates are the baseline for project planning.

2. Manager planning

Planned days

Managers enter planned days per weekly slice. Those rows stay live and can be revised while the week is open or in consolidation.

3. Weekly actuals

Cross-project timesheet

The employee fills the open week with assigned tasks from all projects. Tasks appear even when no days were pre-provisioned.

4. Consolidation

Approval and re-estimation

Submitted timesheets move into the global consolidation queue. PMs approve or reject rows and update the remaining work forecast.

5. Freeze

Snapshot

When the week is closed, the system writes an immutable snapshot with metrics, scope lines and work rows.

Weekly rhythm: the previous week enters consolidation, the current week is open for submission, and future weeks stay planned. At the end of the month, the last week of the month is consolidated, the remaining work for the coming month is checked, and then the closed periods are frozen into a snapshot.

Weekly calendar rhythm

The company-wide submission window is not project-specific
Older weeks

Frozen history

Past weeks are read-only. Their approved actuals already live in snapshots and reporting tables.

Previous week

Consolidation

Employees can no longer submit. PMs validate pending lines and may still adjust future planned days.

Current week

Open for submission

Employees fill and resubmit their weekly timesheet until it is accepted or the week rolls forward.

Future weeks

Planned only

Managers can edit planned days for future weeks. Employees cannot submit actuals until the week opens.

Swimlanes

Employee and manager responsibilities, side by side

Employee POV

Actuals
  1. 1

    See the open week

    The employee sees one weekly timesheet that spans all assigned projects. The sheet is pre-populated with the tasks they are assigned to for that period, even when planned days are zero.

  2. 2

    Create a draft timesheet entry

    Each line starts as DRAFT. It stays editable until the employee submits the weekly sheet for approval.

  3. 3

    Submit

    Submission moves the row to SUBMITTED. From the employee side, the week is now waiting for manager validation.

  4. 4

    Edit and resubmit while the week is open

    If the manager rejects the entry, the employee edits it and resubmits it as many times as needed while the submission window stays open. Past weeks remain read-only.

Manager POV

Planning
  1. 1

    Turn scope into planning

    After quote validation, the manager creates planned days for each weekly period and task/profile pair. Future weeks remain editable.

  2. 2

    Review submitted timesheets

    Submitted weekly timesheets are checked against scope, progress and the remaining forecast. The manager approves or rejects each entry.

  3. 3

    Re-estimate the remaining work

    As approvals accumulate, the PM adjusts future planned days and the remaining work view. That is the live forecast, not the frozen history.

  4. 4

    Start consolidation and freeze

    At week end, the manager closes the consolidated week. At month end, the final consolidated week is closed, the remaining work is verified, and the system writes the snapshot.

Data state machine

What changes, what stays live, what becomes immutable
Global weekly submission window

WeeklySubmissionWindow conceptual

The employee gate is company-wide and weekly, not tied to a single project. The current week is open, the previous week is in consolidation, and older weeks are already frozen.

Live planning

PlannedDay

  • Created and adjusted by the manager.
  • Describes the forecasted load for a project week.
  • Mutates while the week is future, open or in consolidation.
Live actuals

TimesheetEntry

  • Created by the employee as DRAFT on the open weekly sheet.
  • Moves to SUBMITTED, then APPROVED or REJECTED.
  • Approved rows freeze the cost and sell rates that applied at the time.
Consolidation

ProjectPeriodStatus = OPEN → CONSOLIDATION

  • The submission window is closed for employees.
  • The PM validates submitted timesheets and revises the remaining forecast.
  • This is where live planning and actuals are reconciled before the freeze.
Frozen history

Snapshot

  • The freeze transaction creates the snapshot.
  • It stores metrics, scope lines, and work rows for historical reporting.
  • Later edits to planning or rates do not change it.

What each table means

Data ownership and mutability by object
Object Owned by Purpose Mutable until
QuoteLine Manager / commercial Sold scope baseline. Feeds contract value, sold days and rate assumptions. Quote validation or later amendments.
PlannedDay Manager Forecasted work for a project week. Used for capacity planning and remaining work. Period freeze.
TimesheetEntry Employee first, then manager Weekly actual work across the employee's assigned tasks and projects. Captures submitted, approved or rejected time. Approval, rejection, or the weekly submission window closing.
ProjectPeriodStatus Manager / system Controls the project planning period: FUTURE, OPEN, CONSOLIDATION, FROZEN. This is separate from the company-wide employee submission gate. State transition itself.
WeeklySubmissionWindow conceptual System / HR operations Company-wide weekly gate for employee submissions. The current week is open, the previous week is in consolidation, and closed weeks are read-only. Week roll-over or month-end close.
ProfileTaskPeriodStart System at freeze Stores sold and remaining work at the start of the next period. Each new period close.
Snapshot + snapshot children System Immutable historical view used for finance and reporting. Never, once created.

Rule of thumb: planning is predictive, timesheets are weekly actuals, consolidation resolves the gap, and the snapshot locks history.
